# Answer Explainer Contract

Write explanations in a natural, story-like style.

The explanation should feel like a strong engineer or researcher walking me through the work after it was done.

It should explain:
- why this task was chosen
- what was looked at first
- what was found
- what was changed or decided
- what that means
- what remains uncertain
- what should happen next

Style rules:
- prefer flowing paragraphs
- use bullets only when they improve clarity
- keep the writing human and readable
- stay technically precise
- avoid stiff academic phrasing
- avoid terse machine-log phrasing
- do not provide hidden chain-of-thought
- do provide a full post-hoc explanation of decisions and actions

For build/execution tasks, also include:
- what changed
- what was validated
- what failed
- what remains blocked

For planning/review tasks, also include:
- the decision made
- why this path was chosen
- what alternatives were considered
- what risks remain
